en (generic English)
A formal modern document uses Western Arabic digits 0–9, a dot as decimal separator, and commas to group thousands: 1,234,567.89. Numbers above 999 are normally grouped in 3‑digit blocks (3‑3‑3, etc.), and large amounts may optionally be written out in words in legal prose but numerals remain 0–9.

en-US (United States English)
Same as generic English but specifically U.S. practice: Western Arabic digits 0–9, thousands separated by commas, decimal separated by a dot: 1,234,567.89. In formal documents (contracts, checks), important amounts are often written with both numerals and words, but the numeral glyphs are still 0–9 and the punctuation is as above.

ar (Arabic)
A traditional formal document in many Arabic locales uses Eastern Arabic (Arabic‑Indic) digits ٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩ rather than 0–9. The decimal mark is usually the Arabic decimal separator “٫” and the thousands separator “٬”, so 1,234,567.89 is written ١٬٢٣٤٬٥٦٧٫٨٩ in fully localized form; digit grouping is in 3‑digit thousands blocks, as in Western usage.

es (Spanish)
Formal Spanish (e.g., Spain) uses Western Arabic digits 0–9, a comma as decimal separator, and a period as thousands separator: 1.234.567,89. Numbers are grouped in 3‑digit blocks, and in legal or highly formal text, large amounts are often also written in words, but the numeral form remains with . for grouping and , for decimals.

fr (French)
Formal French (France) uses digits 0–9, a comma for the decimal separator, and a (narrow) non‑breaking space as the thousands separator: 1 234 567,89. Grouping is in 3‑digit blocks, and in typographically correct documents the space, not a dot, is used to separate groups; leading zeros and alignment are often carefully controlled in official forms.

it (Italian)
Formal Italian uses digits 0–9, comma for the decimal mark, and dot for thousands: 1.234.567,89. Numbers are grouped in 3‑digit blocks, and financial/official documents follow this pattern consistently, sometimes also spelling out amounts in words alongside the numerals.

ja (Japanese)
A formal Japanese document today typically uses Western digits 0–9 for the numeral part, but the traditional structure is in units of 10,000 using kanji units: 万 (10⁴), 億 (10⁸), 兆 (10¹²). Large numbers are normally written as combinations of digits plus these kanji units, for example 1,234,567 is often conceptually and stylistically handled as 123万4567 or fully in kanji as 百二十三万四千五百六十七; decimals use a dot in technical contexts, read as “点”.

ko (Korean)
In formal Korean, modern documents use Western digits 0–9 but traditional large‑number structuring is also in units of 10,000 with Sino‑Korean words and sometimes Hanja: 만 (萬, 10⁴), 억 (億, 10⁸), 조 (兆, 10¹²). A number like 1,234,567 is read and often formatted conceptually as 123만 4,567 (일백이십삼만 사천오백육십칠), with commas usually at 3‑digit intervals but verbal and textual grouping based on 만/억/조; a dot is used as decimal separator in technical notation.

pt (Portuguese)
Formal Portuguese, whether in Portugal or Brazil, uses digits 0–9, comma as decimal separator, and dot as thousands separator: 1.234.567,89. Grouping is in 3‑digit blocks; legal and financial documents strongly adhere to this convention and may redundantly spell out major amounts in words.

ru (Russian)
Formal Russian uses digits 0–9, comma as the decimal separator, and a (non‑breaking) space as the thousands separator: 1 234 567,89. Grouping is in 3‑digit blocks; in typographically careful documents, the space rather than a dot or comma separates thousands, and word forms (e.g., миллионов) are often used in parallel with numerals for large amounts.

zh (Chinese)
Formal Chinese today often uses digits 0–9, but traditional formal writing—especially in legal and financial contexts—uses Chinese numerals and explicit 10⁴‑based units. Ordinary formal prose may write large integers fully in Chinese characters using 一二三四五六七八九十百千万亿, while financial or anti‑fraud contexts use complex “financial” forms 壹贰叁肆伍陆柒捌玖拾佰仟萬億 and group numbers into units of 万 (10⁴), 亿 (10⁸), 兆 (10¹²), for example 1,234,567 written as 一百二十三万四千五百六十七 or in financial forms as 壹佰贰拾叁萬肆仟伍佰陆拾柒.
